Phir Aayi Hasseen Dillruba: Replicating Sandeep Reddy Vanga’s Storytelling Isn’t Something Taapsee Pannu Film Should Be Proud Of

Post Credits Scene: Taapsee Pannu's Phir Aayi Hasseen Dillruba ends up being a striking example of how even sharp critiques can become entangled in the very narratives they seek to challenge.

The much-anticipated sequel to the intriguing thriller has left audiences and critics in a state of contemplation. While the film attempts to delve deeper into the complexities of human relationships and the darker shades of love, it inadvertently mirrors the storytelling style of Sandeep Reddy Vanga, known for his controversial takes on romance and obsession.

Taapsee Pannu delivers a compelling performance, embodying the nuances of her character with finesse. However, the film's narrative choices raise questions about the glorification of toxic relationships and the thin line between critique and replication.

Director Vinil Mathew seems to grapple with presenting a narrative that critiques societal norms while simultaneously falling prey to the same tropes. The film's aesthetic and thematic elements bear an uncanny resemblance to Vanga’s style, which has been both celebrated and criticized for its portrayal of dysfunctional relationships.

While Phir Aayi Hasseen Dillruba seeks to engage in a conversation about love, betrayal, and obsession, it risks perpetuating the very ideals it aims to scrutinize. The post-credits scene, intended as a meta-commentary, further blurs the lines, leaving the audience pondering the film's true stance.

In an era where cinema holds the power to challenge and reshape narratives, it becomes imperative for filmmakers to navigate these themes with a sense of responsibility. Replicating storytelling styles that border on glorifying toxicity can undermine the film's critical voice, making it a paradoxical piece of art.
